Definitions:

  • First icon:Wildfire Alerts.
  • Second icon:Precipitation Index.
  • Third icon:Wildfire Risk.
  • Municipality and state:The exact location where the alert occurred.
  • Date and time: The exact time and date when the alert occurred.
  • Days without rain: The exact number of days without rainfall in the region.
  • Precipitation: Precipitation refers to any form of water - liquid or solid - that falls from the atmosphere and reaches the ground. This includes rain, snow, sleet, and hail.
  • Fire risk: Fire risk (also known as wildfire risk) refers to the likelihood or probability of a fire starting, spreading, and causing damage in a given area.
  • Biome: A biome is a large geographic area characterized by its climate, soil type, and the types of plants and animals that live there.
  • Average Daytime Temperature: The mean maximum land surface temperature during the daytime within the urban area (in °C).
  • Buffer Average Daytime Temperature: The mean maximum land surface temperature during the daytime in a 10 km buffer zone surrounding the urban area (in °C).
  • Estimated Population in 2000: The estimated number of people living in the urban area in the year 2000.
  • Daytime Temperature Difference: The difference in average daytime maximum temperature between the urban area and the buffer area (in °C).

Wildfires in Rio de Janeiro-São Paulo

Our project introduces an interactive map designed to visualize wildfire hotspots and their significant effects on agriculture in the Rio-SP region. This engaging platform utilizes a rich visual style, enabling users to navigate a detailed landscape that highlights areas affected by wildfires.

Users can explore various layers of data representing wildfire occurrences, including intensity, frequency, and geographic spread. The map allows for dynamic interaction, where users can zoom in on specific regions, filter data by date, and observe the impact on different agricultural zones. This enables a deeper understanding of how wildfires threaten crops and agricultural productivity.

Additionally, the map incorporates visual storytelling elements, showcasing before-and-after scenarios of affected areas to illustrate the devastating consequences of wildfires on the landscape. Users can also access information on soil degradation, crop loss, and economic impacts, presented in an intuitive and visually appealing manner.

By combining immersive visuals with comprehensive data, our project aims to foster awareness of the critical issue of wildfires and their effects on agriculture. This interactive approach not only enhances user engagement but also serves as a powerful tool for educating the public and stakeholders about the urgent need for sustainable land management and wildfire prevention strategies.
